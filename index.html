üéØ How Phishing Attacks Work

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where an attacker tricks the user into giving away sensitive information (such as email, password, OTP) through a fake website or email.

üîó How Phishing Links are Created?

  • The attacker creates a fake website that looks identical to the original one.
  • Then they send the link to the website through various channels‚Äîsuch as email, chat, Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp.
  • The user mistakenly thinks it's a legitimate link and enters their personal information.

üß† Behind the Scene ‚Äì How the Attacker Views the Data?

  • When the user submits their credentials on the fake login page, that data is sent to the attacker's server or script.
  • The attacker logs that data‚Äîsuch as the email, password, IP address, and browser information.
  • In many cases, GeoLocation is also collected along with other data.

üõ°Ô∏è How to Stay Safe?

  • Before clicking any link, check if it starts with: https://.
  • Verify the spelling of the URL ‚Äì small spelling mistakes can indicate a fake site.
  • Always enable 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication) on all your accounts.
  • If your browser or app warns you, don't ignore it.
